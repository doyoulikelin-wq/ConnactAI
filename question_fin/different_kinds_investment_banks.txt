Different Kinds of Investment Banks (Summary)

Note: Credit Suisse is intentionally excluded (as requested).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1) Bulge Bracket (Full-Service Global Investment Banks)
What they typically do:
- Provide “full service” to large corporations and institutional clients.
- Underwrite equity and debt (e.g., IPOs, follow-on offerings, bond issuance).
- Advise on large (often billion-dollar) M&A transactions.
- note: Many also run large Sales & Trading platforms and broad capital markets capabilities.

Examples shown:
- Goldman Sachs
- Morgan Stanley
- J.P. Morgan
- Citi
- Bank of America Merrill Lynch
- UBS
- Barclays
- Deutsche Bank

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2) Commercial Banks with Investment Banking (IB) Capabilities
What they typically do:
- “Powered by the balance sheet” (strong lending / financing base).
- Often competitive in middle-market advisory and equity offerings.
- May occasionally participate in high-profile public mandates.

Examples shown:
- RBC
- HSBC
- Wells Fargo
- Mizuho
- BNP Paribas
- BMO
- TD Securities

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3) Middle Market Investment Banks
What they typically do:
- Deal sizes generally smaller than Bulge Bracket transactions.
- Mandates can include both M&A advisory and capital markets work.
- Strong presence in sponsor-backed and mid-sized corporate transactions.

Examples shown:
- Jefferies
- William Blair
- Piper Sandler
- Raymond James
- Cowen

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4) Boutiques (Advisory-Focused Firms)
What they typically do:
- Typically focus on advisory (especially M&A and restructuring).
- Often high deal flow; teams can be highly specialized.
- Many boutiques do not underwrite IPOs (limited or no balance sheet / underwriting platform).

Examples shown:
- Lazard
- Rothschild
- LionTree
- Evercore
- PJT
- Greenhill
- Moelis
- Guggenheim
- Robey Warshaw
- Perella Weinberg Partners
